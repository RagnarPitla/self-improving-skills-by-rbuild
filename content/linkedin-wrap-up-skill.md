# LinkedIn Post: Wrap-Up Skill

---

I've built dozens of Claude Code skills.

The one I actually use every session? A glorified checklist.

It's called wrap-up. Four phases, fully automated:

**Ship It** - commits code, pushes to remote, organizes misplaced files. Catches the "I never committed that" problem.

**Remember It** - reviews the session for learnable moments. Saves patterns to Claude's memory hierarchy. Over weeks, your setup gets smarter without effort.

**Review & Apply** - Claude reviews its own mistakes. "You asked me to do X three times. I should do that automatically." Then it writes the rule and commits it.

**Publish It** - flags content worth sharing, drafts it, saves it. No more good ideas lost in conversations I'll never reopen.

The meta point:

The best skills aren't impressive. They're the ones that run boring routines you'd skip.

Every session that ends with /wrap-up leaves my projects more organized, my Claude setup smarter, and occasionally produces a post I didn't plan to write.

AI compounds when you close the loop.

Full skill is open source: github.com/RagnarPitla/self-improving-skills
